Key Authors & Their Contributions
1. Amisha Sharma, Rajneet Kaur Bijral, Jatinder Manhas, Vinod Sharma

Study: Mango Leaf Diseases Detection using Deep Learning.

Method: CNN-based approach using data augmentation to identify Anthracnose, Red rust, and Powdery mildew.

Result: Achieved ~90.36% accuracy. 
Academia

2. Teena Varma, Prajwal Mate, Noamaan Abdul Azeem, Sanjeev Sharma, Bhupendra Singh

Study: Automatic mango leaf disease detection using different transfer learning models.

Models: VGG19, InceptionV3, ResNet152V2, DenseNet121, InceptionResNetV2, MobileNetV2, Xception.

Highlight: InceptionV3 achieved 99.87% accuracy. 
SpringerLink

3. Rabia Saleem, Jamal Hussain Shah, Muhammad Sharif, Mussarat Yasmin, Hwan-Seung Yong, Jaehyuk Cha

Study: Mango Leaf Disease Recognition and Classification using Novel Segmentation and Vein Pattern Technique.

Technique: Leaf vein segmentation, CCA-based feature fusion, and cubic SVM classifier.

Result: 95.5% accuracy. 
MDPI

4. Tanishta, Vishesh Goyal, Megha Arakeri

Study: Identifying Mango Leaf Diseases with Advanced Deep Learning Approaches and Convolutional Neural Networks, presented at INCIP 2025.

Method: EfficientNet model with ~12,046 images covering 9 classes (1 healthy + 8 disease types).

Performance: Accuracy 98.97%, F1 Score 0.9960. 
Manipal Academy

5. Sarder Iftekhar Ahmed, Muhammad Ibrahim, Md. Nadim, Md. Mizanur Rahman, Maria Mehjabin Shejunti, Taskeed Jabid, Md. Sawkat Ali

Dataset: MangoLeafBD – A Comprehensive Image Dataset to Classify Diseased and Healthy Mango Leaves.

Contribution: Created a publicly available dataset of 4,000 images across seven diseases (from Bangladesh).

Authors: A. Ahmed et al. 
arXiv

6. Rafi Hassan Chowdhury, Sabbir Ahmed

Study: MangoLeafViT: Leveraging Lightweight Vision Transformer with Runtime Augmentation for Efficient Mango Leaf Disease Classification, May 2025.

Approach: Lightweight Vision Transformer (ViT) architecture with runtime data augmentation.

Results: 99.43% accuracy on the MangoLeafBD dataset with computational efficiency. 
arXiv
